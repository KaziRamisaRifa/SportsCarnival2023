Rules & Regulation

Cricket

1. All the players in the squad must be enrolled students of their respective universities.
2. The team has to submit a photocopy of the ID card, a photocopy of the advising slip, and a passport-size photo of each player in the squad with the registration form.
3. Within Aguest 30, 2023 each team has to submit the squad list of 15 players with their required documents.
4. Each side will play ten overs in a match, which should end in a maximum of 45 minutes.
5. First, Three overs are considered a powerplay, and only two fielders allow fielding outside the 30-yard circle. Additionally, each bowler has allowed Bowling two overs in each match.
6. Moreover, if the match draws between 2 teams, it will go for super over cricket. It means each team will play one over. However, who score high in 1 over will be considered a Winner. Even if we don't find a winner through one super over, the process will continue until we find a winner.
7. The DLS method will be applied if 25% of a match is played and stops due to any circumstances. In case of unavoidable interruption in a match, a minimum of five over the game has to be played to find a result from the game. However, if the game is not played due to unavoidable circumstances, the teams will divide the points.
8. Umpires will make decisions in any situation, and their decision will be the final decision.
9. Two points for the winning and zero for the losing sides in the group stage. However, each team will get 1 point in case of a tie or postponement due to any reason.
10.Due to match-fixing cases, management will be allowed to take any action against it.
11.Every team has to report 45 minutes before their match time.
12.There can be up to three professional players playing in the best eleven for each team. For a 15-man squad, there can be up to four professional players. Players registered in the national, premier, U-19 national, and 1st, 2nd, and 3rd divisions will be considered professionals in the tournament.
13.The organizing management only holds the right to change, deduct or add any rules if necessary. 14.The rest of the rules will be followed according to the MCC cricket rules.

Football

1. All the players in the squad must be enrolled students of their respective universities.
2. The team has to submit a photocopy of the ID card, a photocopy of the advising slip, and a passport-size photo of each player in the squad.
3. Within Aguest 30, 2023, each team has to submit the squad list of 15 players with their required documents.
4. A match consists of two 35 minutes halves with a 10-minute rest period in between.
5. The match referee's call is the Final call. In a match, the Referee acts as a timekeeper and makes any decisions that may need to be made, such as fouls, free kicks, throw-ins, penalties, and added-on time at the end of each half. The Referee may consult the assistant Match referees at any time in the match regarding a decision.
6. After the group stage, if a game finishes without the results, it will directly convert into a penalty shootout. In contrast, the Referee operates the shootout according to the international act.
7. The whole ball must cross the goal line to constitute a goal.
8. For fouls committed, a player could receive either a yellow or red card depending on the severity of the foul; this comes down to the Referee's discretion. The yellow is a warning, and the red card is a dismissal of that player. Two yellow cards will equal one red. Once a player is sent off, then they cannot be replaced. If a player receives two yellow cards in back-to- back matches, he will not play in the next match, but it will be dismissed into the next round of the tournament, like Quarter, Semi & final.
9. If a ball goes out of play off an opponent on either of the sidelines, then it is given as a throw-in. It is a goal kick if it goes out of play off an attacking player on the baseline. If it comes off a defending player, it is a corner kick.
10.The squad limit for each team will be 15, and one manager is mandatory for each university. The manager will be in direct contact with the organizers.
11.Each team can have a maximum of 9 players (including one goalkeeper, the only player allowed to handle the ball within the 18-yard box), and a minimum of 6 players are needed to constitute a match.
12.There can be up to three professional players playing in the best nine for each team. For a 15-man squad, there can be up to four professional players.
13. Players registered in the BPL and BCL after 2021 will be considered professionals in the tournament. 14. If any player gets injured and ruled out of the tournament while the tournament is going on, he can only be replaced with a regular player. For a win in group stages, 3 points will be awarded to the winning, 1 point for a draw, and 0 for a loss.
15. The team's presence should be reported to the organizer 10 minutes before the match.
16. Teams failing to be present in the field on match time will be penalized through a walkover, i.e., the opposition will get a walkover. If both teams fail to be present on time, both teams will get 0 points from that match.
17. Suppose multiple teams finish with the same number of points in the group stages. In that case, goal difference will be considered, followed by goals for goals conceded, head-to-head, cards conceive, and penalty shootout, respectively.
18. For the group stages match ending with no result will be considered a draw.
19. For Knockout stages, a match ending with no results in the expected time will go directly to penalties, with each team taking five penalties, after which sudden death will occur.
20. Offside rules will be applied.
21. Players must wear adequate shin protection before entering the field.
22. Players must wear Boots. Turf or Barefoot is strictly not allow in the field.
23. Under certain circumstances, organizers can call if necessary.
24. Teams will be able to make five substitutions. Each team will be given just three in-game opportunities to make changes. But they also get an additional sixth option if the game goes into extra time (after 30 minutes). If a player is substituted once, they cannot be substituted again during the entire match.
25. The organizing management only holds the right to change, deduct or add any rules if necessary.
25. The rest of the rules will follow the BFF's rules.

Handball

In handball, a goal is scored when the whole of the ball passes between the goalposts and travels entirely over the goal line. However, a goal is only awarded once the Referee has signaled this, and they are confident that no rules have been broken. At the end of the match, the team with the most goals will be awarded the winners. However, a draw is recorded if both teams have the same score.
1. All the players in the squad must be enrolled students of their respective universities.
2. The team has to submit a photocopy of the ID card, a photocopy of the advising slip, and a passport-size photo of each player in the squad.
3. Within February 14, 2023 each team has to submit the squad list of 10 players with their required documents.
4. A competitive game consists of equal 30-minute halves with a 10-minute break.
5. A team must attempt to attack to keep possession of the ball.
6. The start is awarded to the team that wins the coin toss.
7. A match begins with both teams in their half.
8. A goal can be scored from any throw.
9. A player can run with the ball for three steps maximum.
10. A player can hold a ball for up to three seconds maximum.
11. A player can continuously dribble, providing they bounce the ball.
12. A player can take three steps maximum before and after dribbling (no 'double dribble').
13. Players are not able to endanger an opponent with the ball.
14. Players are not permitted to pull, hit or punch the ball out of an opponent's hands.
15. Players cannot make contact with the ball below the knee.
16. Players cannot dive on the floor to regain a loose ball.
17. A player is allowed to use the torso of the body to obstruct an opponent with or without the ball.
18. A player cannot outstretch arms or legs to obstruct, push, hold, trip, or hit.
19. An attacking player is not allowed to charge into a defensive player. 20. A throw-in is awarded when the ball goes out of bounds, and the thrower must place one foot on the sideline to execute the throw.
21. All opposing players must stay 3 m away from the throw-in.
22. All minor fouls or violations are penalized with the awarding of a free throw taken at the place of infringement.

Volleyball

1. All the players in the squad must be enrolled students of their respective universities.
2. The team has to submit a photocopy of the ID card, a photocopy of the advising slip, and a passport-size photo of each player in the squad.
3. Within February 14, 2023 each team has to submit the squad list of 10 players with their required documents.
4. Only six players on the floor at any given time: 3 in the front row and 3 in the back row.
5. All matches will be played for three sets, with 25 points for each set. The final match will be played for five sets, with the first four sets scoring 25 points each and the last set scoring 15 points.
6. Points are made on every service for the winning team of the rally (rally- point scoring).
7. Players may not hit the ball twice in succession (a block is not considered a hit).
8. The ball may be played off the net during a volley and on a serve.
9. A ball hitting a boundary line is in.
10. A ball is out if it hits an antenna, the floor outside the court, any net or cables outside the antennae, the Referee stands or pole, or the ceiling above a non- playable area.
11. Contacting the ball with any part of a player's body is legal.
12. It is illegal to catch, hold or throw the ball.
13. A player cannot block or attack a serve on or inside the 10-foot line.
14. After the serve, front-line players may switch positions at the net.
15. Matches are made up of sets; the number depends on the level of play.
